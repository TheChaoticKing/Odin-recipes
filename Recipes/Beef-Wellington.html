Beef Wellington

It is a classic English dish of beef tenderloin coated in a mushroom pâté, wrapped in puff pastry, and baked

Beef Wellington can be time-consuming and difficult to make. The tenderloin needs to be cooked until fully done but not overdone, while the puff pastry needs to stay light and airy.

Ingredients:

Duxelle

  • 1 1/2 punds of white button mushroom
  • 2 shallots, roughly chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, roughly chopped
  • 2 Tbsp of unsalted butter
  • 2 Tbsp of extra virgin olive oil
  • Kosher salt and ground pepper

The Beef

  • 3 pounds of center cut beef tenderloin
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • Kosher salt and ground pepper
  • 12 thin slices of prosciutto
  • 6 sprigs of fresh Thyme
  • 2 Tbsp of Dijon Mustard
  • flour
  • 1 pound of Puff pastry
  • 2 large eggs [lightly beaten]
  • 1/2 tsp of sea salt

Steps:

Duxelle:

  1. Add the mushrooms, shallots, garlic, and thyme to a food processer and pulse it until finely chopped
  2. Add butter and olive oil to a large sauce pan on medium heat.
  3. Add the mushroom/shallot mixture and saute for 8-10 min until evaperated
  4. Season with salt and peeper and set it aside to cool

The beef

  1. Tie the tenderloin in 4 places to hold its shape while cooking.
  2. Drizzle it with olive and then season with salt/pepper then sear all all over [including ends], in a hot skillet coated in olive oil for about 2-3 min
  3. Set the prosciutto on a sheet of plastic wrapped [about a foot long]. Shingle it to form a large rectangle to wrap fully around the beef
  4. With a rubber spatula, spread the duxelle over the prosciutto and season with salt and pepper.
  5. When Beef is sheared, remove it from heat, cut off the twine, and smear it with the dijon mustard
  6. Allow to cool slightly, then roll up in the duxelles covered prosciutto using the plastic wrap to tie it up nice and tigh
  7. Roll it up tightly in plastic wrap and twist the ends to seal it completely and hold it in a nice log shape. Set in the refrigerator for 30 minutes to ensure it maintains its shape.
  8. Preheat oven 435 degrees F
  9. On a lightly floured surface, roll the puff pastry out to about a 1/4-inch thickness. You may have to over lap 2 sheets
  10. Remove beef from refrigerator and cut off plastic. Set the beef in the center of the pastry and fold over the longer sides, brushing with egg wash to seal
  11. Top with coarse sea salt. Place the beef seam side down on a baking sheet.
  12. Brush the top of the pastry with egg wash then make a couple of slits in the top of the pastry using the tip of a paring knife
  13. Bake for 40 to 45 minutes until pastry is golden brown and beef registers 125 degrees F on an instant-read thermometer
  14. Remove from oven and rest before cutting into thick slices
