Donuts

A sweet, ring-shaped snack food that's typically deep-fried and made from flour dough

Ingredients:

The dough

  • 1 3/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 3/4 tsp kosher salt
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 cup buttermilk
  • 1/4 cup flavorless oil
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup water
  • Sprinkles for decorating
  • Glaze

Blood orange glaze

  • 3 cups confectioners sugar
  • 2 Tbsp honey
  • 4 to 5 tablespoons fresh blood orange juice

Steps:

The dough

  1. preheat the oven to 375 degrees F. Coat a 12-cavity donut pan with cooking spray and set aside
  2. Whisk together the flour, sugar, salt, baking powder and baking soda in a large bowl
  3. Whisk together the egg, buttermilk, oil, vanilla and water in a medium bowl
  4. Whisk the wet mixture into the dry mixture and stir to combine
  5. Fill a piping bag with the batter and pipe the batter into the donut pans, filling each cavity halfway
  6. Bake until a toothpick inserted into a donut comes out clean, about 12 minutes. Cool in the pan for 5 minutes. Remove to a rack and cool completely.
  7. To decorate the donuts, dip them halfway into the glaze and then allow excess glaze to drip off. Sprinkle the tops with sprinkles

The glaze

  1. Combine the confectioners' sugar, honey and 4 tablespoons of the juice in a bowl
  2. Add more juice little by little until the mixture is spreadable
  3. It should be quite thick yet spreadable.
